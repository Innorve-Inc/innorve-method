# Brand Use Guide

Practical guidance for using Innorve marks in honest reference. Companion to [`TRADEMARK.md`](TRADEMARK.md).

If you are publishing, building, teaching, hiring, citing, or speaking about the Innorve Method, this document tells you what is welcomed, what requires permission, and what is not allowed. The full legal framework is in `TRADEMARK.md`; this is the practical version.

---

## The five-second rule

Before using an Innorve mark, ask yourself: **"Would a reader assume my work is endorsed by, certified by, or affiliated with Innorve Academy?"**

- If **no**, you are likely in the safe zone of nominative fair use.
- If **yes**, you need permission.

---

## Welcomed without permission

These uses make the Innorve Method more useful in the world. Please use them.

### In code, documentation, and technical writing

- *"This service implements the Innorve Skill Contract Schema (IM-04)."*
- *"We use the Capability Graph Format from the Innorve Method to publish our team's quarterly AI maturity report."*
- *"Our agent runtime validates skill manifests against the Innorve Maturity Gate Model."*

### In job descriptions and hiring

- *"Familiarity with the Innorve Method is a plus."*
- *"Comfort authoring Skill Contracts (IM-04) preferred."*
- *"Bonus: candidate has progressed to L3 on the Innorve Architect Ladder."*

You may **not** state that you are looking for a "Certified Innorve Architect" unless you intend to verify the credential against the Innorve Academy directory at innorve.academy/architects. The credential designation is reserved.

### In academic and journalistic work

- Citing the Innorve Method in a paper, article, blog post, or conference talk
- Quoting from any document in this repository, with attribution
- Comparing the Innorve Method to other methodologies in critical analysis
- Critiquing or disagreeing with any framework or specification

### In teaching and training

- Teaching the Innorve Method in a university course, internal training program, or workshop, with attribution
- Including IM-XX framework references in your own course materials
- Distributing the open Innorve Method skills to your students via the GitHub repository
- Pointing learners at innorve.academy/method as a primary reference

You may **not** offer credentials using the Innorve Architect, Certified Innorve Architect, or Innorve Fellow designations. Those certifications are issued only by Innorve Academy.

### In conference talks and public speaking

- Naming the Innorve Method in your talk title, abstract, slides, or materials
- Walking an audience through any IM-XX framework
- Demonstrating the Innorve Method skills in a live setting
- Referencing the Innorve Architect Ladder

When you do, please link back to innorve.academy/method so listeners can find the canonical source.

## Requires permission

The activities below require an executed written agreement with Innorve, Inc. Email **legal@innorve.ai** to start the conversation.

### Issuing credentials

- Awarding any badge, certificate, diploma, or formal recognition that uses any reserved Innorve mark
- Operating a parallel credentialing program under any Innorve-prefixed name
- Issuing certificates of completion for a course you call "Innorve Method Training" or similar

### Branded products and services

- Selling a software product whose name contains an Innorve mark
- Offering "Innorve Method Consulting," "Innorve Architect Services," or any service whose marketing relies on a reserved mark
- Building a SaaS platform whose name suggests official affiliation
- Operating a domain that contains "innorve" in any form

### Joint promotion

- Co-branded marketing of any kind
- Speaking on behalf of Innorve at events
- Operating booths or spaces at conferences using Innorve identity
- Press statements that quote or imply Innorve endorsement

---

## Not allowed under any circumstance

These uses fragment the credential, mislead the public, or damage the integrity of the methodology. They are not negotiable.

- Calling yourself, your team, or your company an "Innorve Architect," "Certified Innorve Architect," "Innorve Fellow," or "Innorve Academy graduate" without holding the actual credential
- Publishing a competing "Innorve Method v2," "Next-Gen Innorve Method," "Innorve Method Pro," or any variant suggesting a parallel canonical version
- Reproducing the Innorve visual identity (logos, badge artwork, official typography lockups) in your own materials
- Selling certifications, badges, or completion credentials under any Innorve mark
- Implying that Innorve has reviewed, certified, or endorsed your AI system, your code, your team, or your training program when no such review has occurred
- Registering confusable domains, social handles, or organization names (e.g., `inn0rve`, `innorve-architects`, `innorvemethodpro`)
- Misrepresenting your relationship to Innorve in any commercial or professional context

## Special note for tool vendors

If you build a developer tool, IDE plugin, runtime, validator, or similar that implements one of the Innorve Specs, you are welcome and encouraged to do so. To be listed on the public spec implementations page at innorve.academy/spec, file a "Spec Implementation" issue at github.com/Innorve-Inc/innorve-method/issues.

You may state in your tool's documentation: *"This tool implements the [Spec Name] from the Innorve Method."*

You may **not** state: *"Certified by Innorve Academy"* unless you have entered into a written certification agreement.

## Special note for university and college programs

If you teach the Innorve Method in a CS, business, design, or executive education program, please:

1. Attribute the methodology to Innorve Academy in course materials
2. Link to innorve.academy/method as the canonical reference
3. Do not award an "Innorve Architect" credential at the end of the course (that designation is reserved)
4. Optional: contact us about the Innorve Academy University Partnership Program, which provides licensed curriculum, dual credentialing, and a placement pipeline (academic@innorve.ai)

---

## Special note for graduates and certified architects

If you have completed an Innorve Academy program and earned a credential, you have a separate, narrower license to use the relevant designation in your professional materials. The terms are issued with the credential.

You **may**:

- State your tier on LinkedIn, your resume, your professional bio (e.g., "Innorve Architect, Cohort 1," "Certified Innorve Architect, 2026")
- Display the Innorve Architect badge issued to you on your own personal site
- List the credential in talks, articles, and professional communications

You **may not**:

- Issue credentials to others using your designation
- Imply that your work or your employer's work is endorsed by Innorve simply because you hold the credential
- Use the badge artwork outside the contexts described in your credential terms
- Continue using the designation after revocation, expiration, or non-renewal

## Why this matters to you

If you are a serious adopter of the Innorve Method, the brand discipline is in your interest as much as ours.

A credential that anyone can claim is worth nothing. A methodology that fragments into competing "Pro" versions becomes useless as a shared standard. A name that means anything means nothing.

By respecting the trademark separation, you protect the value of the methodology you have invested time learning, the credential you may eventually pursue, and the institutional integrity that allows the Innorve Architect designation to mean something to the people who hire and audit your work.

That is the contract: open methodology, protected brand, durable credential.
